# Segment 1: Developers & Project Sponsors

**Revenue Potential:** Highest

This is the primary fee-generating segment. Developers and project sponsors pay for advisory services on transaction structuring, CDE sourcing, application support, and compliance management. A single NMTC or HTC engagement can generate $50K–$250K+ in advisory fees depending on project complexity.

## Who They Are

Real estate developers (for-profit and nonprofit), community health center operators, charter school networks, manufacturers expanding into new facilities, and nonprofit organizations undertaking capital projects in low-income census tracts. They range from experienced repeat users of tax credits to first-timers who have heard about NMTCs or HTCs but don't know where to start.

On LinkedIn, they are VP-to-C-suite level at development firms, executive directors of nonprofits, or project managers at CDFIs. They follow industry organizations (Urban Land Institute, Main Street America, National Trust) and consume content during morning commutes and lunch breaks.

## Biggest Frustrations

- **The process feels impenetrable.** They've been told NMTCs could bring 15–25% in project subsidy, but every explanation they find is written in acronym-laden jargon that assumes they already understand the structure. They feel locked out of a program that was designed for them.
- **CDE selection is opaque.** With 142 organizations receiving allocation in the latest round, choosing the right CDE partner feels like navigating a black box. They don't know which CDEs align with their geography, project type, or timeline.
- **Timeline uncertainty.** NMTC transactions typically take 6–12 months to close. HTC projects require NPS certification that can add months. Developers working with lenders and construction schedules need predictability they rarely get.
- **Cost of professional services feels prohibitive for smaller projects.** Legal, accounting, and advisory fees on a twinned NMTC/HTC transaction can run $300K–$500K, which is disproportionate for projects under $5 million.
- **Compliance anxiety.** The seven-year NMTC compliance period and five-year HTC recapture period create ongoing monitoring obligations that feel burdensome, especially for organizations without dedicated compliance staff.

## Content That Makes Them Follow, Engage, and Buy

**Follow triggers:**
- Educational carousels that explain how NMTC or HTC transactions work, step by step, without assuming prior knowledge.
- Before/after project photos with a brief narrative of how the financing came together.
- "Eligibility checker" style posts: "Does your project qualify for NMTCs? Here are the 4 questions to ask."

**Engagement triggers:**
- "Common mistakes" posts that validate their anxieties: "The #1 Reason First-Time NMTC Projects Stall (And How to Avoid It)."
- Polls and questions: "What's the biggest barrier to using tax credits in your projects?" This gives them a low-stakes way to signal interest.
- Posts that name specific project types: "If you're a community health center, here's why NMTCs should be on your radar."

**Conversion triggers:**
- Case studies with financial specifics: "How a $12M mixed-use project in Appalachia used twinned NMTCs and HTCs to close a $3.1M financing gap."
- Direct offers of value: "We put together a free NMTC eligibility checklist. DM us and we'll send it."
- Proof of process: content that demonstrates Galvanize's methodology, not just outcomes. Developers buy process confidence.

# Segment 3: CDEs (Community Development Entities)

**Revenue Potential:** Moderate (Allocation & Compliance)

CDEs receive NMTC allocation from the CDFI Fund and deploy it into qualifying projects. They may pay for advisory support on allocation strategy, application narrative, compliance program design, and deployment pipeline development. Revenue potential is moderate but relationship value is high—CDEs are gatekeepers to deal flow.

## Who They Are

Certified CDEs including CDFIs, community development arms of banks, specialized allocation recipients, and mission-driven lenders. On LinkedIn, they are allocation directors, compliance officers, and executive leadership at CDEs. They monitor CDFI Fund announcements, Novogradac, and peer CDEs.

## Biggest Frustrations

- **Allocation competition.** The CDFI Fund receives far more applications than it can fund. CDEs face intense competition and need to differentiate their applications and deployment track records.
- **Deployment pressure.** Allocation must be deployed within timelines; finding qualifying projects that align with mission and geography is a constant challenge.
- **Compliance complexity.** Post-2025 reforms have raised the bar for monitoring and reporting. CDEs fear decertification, loss of allocation, and recapture of past awards for non-compliance.
- **CDE certification disruptions.** The CDFI Fund paused new CDE certification applications from June to September 2025, creating uncertainty for organizations seeking to enter the market.
- **Differentiation in a crowded field.** With hundreds of certified CDEs, standing out in allocation applications and to project sponsors is an ongoing challenge.

## Content That Makes Them Follow, Engage, and Buy

**Follow triggers:**
- Allocation round analysis: who won, how much, what patterns emerge in geography, project type, and application strategy.
- Regulatory tracking: CDFI Fund policy changes, compliance guidance, and certification updates.

**Engagement triggers:**
- "Lessons from the allocation round" posts that offer specific, non-obvious takeaways from the latest results. CDEs are hungry for any competitive intelligence that improves their next application.
- Posts about innovative transaction structures (twinning, layered financing, challenging project types) that CDEs can learn from.

**Conversion triggers:**
- Offers to review allocation application narratives or deployment strategy. CDEs will engage with advisors who demonstrate allocation-round expertise.
- Compliance readiness assessments: "The CDFI Fund just raised the bar. Let us review your compliance infrastructure before the next monitoring cycle."

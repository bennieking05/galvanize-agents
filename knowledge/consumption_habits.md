# Content Consumption Habits by Segment

Use this when scheduling posts and choosing formats. Match content to when each segment is online and how they consume.

| Habit | Developers | Investors | CDEs | Community |
|-------|-------------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Primary platform** | LinkedIn | LinkedIn | LinkedIn + Email | LinkedIn + Facebook |
| **Peak consumption time** | 7–9am, 12–1pm | 7–8am, 5–7pm | Throughout workday | Evenings, weekends |
| **Preferred format** | Carousels, case studies | Long-form text, data charts | Articles, regulatory summaries | Short video, photo stories |
| **Scroll vs. seek behavior** | Seek (search for solutions) | Scroll (absorb intel passively) | Seek + scroll (monitor ecosystem) | Scroll (stumble on content) |
| **Trust signals** | Case studies, specificity | Data, credentials, portfolio | Peer CDE endorsements | Community testimonials |
| **Engagement style** | Save, DM, share privately | Comment, debate, reshare | Comment, tag colleagues | Like, share to personal network |
| **Conversion path** | DM → call → engagement | Content → report → meeting | Conference → follow-up | Webinar → intro call |

## Implications for Galvanize

- **Developers:** Post educational and case-study content in the morning or at lunch. Use carousels and concrete numbers. CTAs that invite DMs perform well.
- **Investors:** Post market/policy content early morning or late afternoon. Lead with data and a clear point of view. Gated reports and meetings are the conversion path.
- **CDEs:** Keep a steady stream of regulatory and allocation content. They check throughout the day. Conferences and direct follow-up drive conversion.
- **Community:** Post impact stories and simple explainers in the evening or on weekends. Use photos and short video. Webinars and low-commitment intro calls convert.

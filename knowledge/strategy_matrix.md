# Cross-Segment Content Strategy Matrix

The following matrix maps specific post types to each segment's engagement pattern, allowing Galvanize to create content that serves multiple audiences simultaneously.

| Post Type | Developers | Investors | CDEs | Community |
|-----------|------------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Impact spotlight (project case study) | FOLLOW + BUY | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + ENGAGE |
| Educational carousel (how credits work) | FOLLOW | Low | Low | FOLLOW |
| Policy/legislative update | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + ENGAGE | FOLLOW + ENGAGE | Low |
| Market pricing/data analysis | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + BUY | ENGAGE | Low |
| "Common mistakes" post | FOLLOW + ENGAGE | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | ENGAGE |
| Allocation round analysis | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + BUY | Low |
| Community voice / testimonial | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + ENGAGE |
| Contrarian take / opinion post | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + ENGAGE | ENGAGE | Low |
| Free resource offer (checklist, guide) | BUY | BUY | BUY | FOLLOW + BUY |
| Compliance deep-dive | ENGAGE | ENGAGE | FOLLOW + BUY | Low |

## Reading the Matrix

- **FOLLOW** = This post type is most likely to earn a new follower from this segment.
- **ENGAGE** = This post type drives comments, shares, or DMs from this segment.
- **BUY** = This post type is most likely to lead to an inbound advisory inquiry.
- **Low** = This segment is unlikely to engage meaningfully with this post type.

## Use in Planning

When planning a content calendar, choose post types that maximize FOLLOW and BUY for the segments you are targeting that week. Use ENGAGE-focused post types to maintain visibility with segments that are not the primary target. Avoid post types marked Low for a segment unless you have a specific reason (e.g., brand consistency).
